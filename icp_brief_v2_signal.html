Signal vs Noise — ICP Brief v2

v1 had a selection problem. It weighted a broke, fearful, declined prospect (Anthony) as if he were the target. He isn't — he's noise. This rebuild separates signal from noise and profiles the high-agency buyer worth 10x the attention: the Olivia type.

1 · The correction

If you build the funnel around the people who didn't buy and couldn't pay, you build a funnel that attracts more people who won't buy and can't pay. v1's headline rec — "move financing earlier" — is a noise-driven optimization. It lowers the bar to convert more Anthonys.

The opposite move is the real one: raise the bar and repel the noise, so the funnel fills with Olivias — people for whom $5,800 PIF is a Tuesday, who decide in a weekend, and who actually execute. Fewer, better. The job of intelligence isn't to describe everyone who showed up. It's to find who's worth showing up for.

2 · The signal-vs-noise SOP

Score every prospect on these markers before a closer's time is spent. 3+ signal markers → fast-track. 3+ noise markers → free community / nurture, do not burn the close.

Wants brutal honesty, a system to lock into, intensity. "I love a challenge."Wants reassurance and safety more than a challenge
Self-selected via the paid ad and booked directlyPrice-shopping, "free resources first"

4 · The signal ICP — "the creative in a corporate cage"

A high-income professional (tech, sales, corporate) with a disowned creative identity. 24–40. Resourced. The job pays the bills and kills the soul. UGC isn't about money — it's the practical vehicle to fund the art and buy back the self.

What she actually wants

  • Time + financial freedom — "do what I want when I want, from the beach"
  • To stop compromising artistic integrity for money
  • A system to lock into that cuts the noise — not more information
  • A mentor with intensity she trusts — picked Gloria for exactly this
  • Someone to "gaslight me into being confident and tell me exactly what to do"
  • Brutal honest feedback — "tell me my video sucks," not flowery praise

How she buys (and why it's different)

  • Money isn't the gate — she earns. The gate is trust + does this cut the noise
  • Decides fast and pays in full — agency is the through-line
  • Buys the mentor's intensity + the system, not the income claim
  • Quit threshold $5k/mo · dream $15k/mo · wants out by her birthday
  • Came from a paid ad (saw Gloria), self-selected, booked direct
  • Has a build history (book, protein-pudding biz, freelance) — she ships

7 · Bets to attract more signal (and repel noise)

H1 · angle

If high-earning creatives in respectable jobs see a "you're not broke, you're caged — the open mic is the church" identity angle, then signal-lead share rises and DQ rate drops.

H2 · qualifier

If the Typeform raises the income/agency bar (already-earning, has-built-before) instead of leading with budget, then closer time concentrates on PIF-likely prospects and CPBC-to-cash improves.

H3 · message

If ads sell Gloria's intensity + "a system that cuts the noise" rather than income claims, then the prospects who book self-select toward high agency.

H4 · hook

If a cold ad opens with "I needed someone to gaslight me into confidence and tell me exactly what to do" (Olivia's own words), it will magnetize her twin and bore the tire-kicker.

8 · What I'd act on

  • Re-cut the qualifier to score agency, not budget. "Are you currently earning?" + "What have you built before?" beats "What's your budget?" The first finds Olivias; the second finds Anthonys.
  • Make the hero angle identity, not income. "You're not broke — you're caged." Sell reclaiming the disowned creative self. Money claims attract the desperate; identity attracts the resourced.
  • Lead with Gloria's intensity as the differentiator. Signal buyers pick the mentor, not the curriculum. "I'll tell you your video sucks" is a feature to Olivia, a threat to Anthony — that's the filter working.
  • Reverse v1: don't rush to financing. Financing is a noise-magnet. PIF buyers are the signal. Protect the price; let it repel.
  • Tag every closed customer Signal / Noise going forward. Build the bot's training set so it learns to predict agency from the application alone — that's the compounding edge.
