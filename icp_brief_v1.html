1 · The sharpest ICP in the data

The highest-intent prospect right now is the recently laid-off corporate escapee, 35–58, who needs to replace an income they just lost and is done with the commute-and-cubicle life. They are motivated and financially tight at the same time — the layoff is both the trigger and the obstacle. They do not want "another business." They want a skill, a structure, and someone to hand-hold them because a past course or attempt already let them down.

2 · Income target clusters at $3–5k/mo

~10 / 26
"Replace my income / be comfortable" is the number, not "get rich." A few dreamers ($10–30k) but the center of mass is modest and concrete.

3 · #1 objection is money — caused by the same layoff that motivates them

~10 / 26
The cruel irony of the ICP: the layoff makes them desperate to buy and unable to pay. This is why financing and payment plans are load-bearing, not optional.
Evidence: April, Chelle, Maricelle ($8k car repairs), Liliana ($3k cap), Kirk (can't pay today), Cindy ($500 vs $1–2k). All 5 closes used a plan — Logan ($2k down + pause), Cindy (ClarityPay $399×18), Chloe ($1k/mo), Michelle ($2k down + plan).

4 · Beneath "money" sits fear and lack of self-belief

~5 / 26
"Is this real for ME?" The shadow objection. They cite money because it's socially acceptable; the real block is fear of failing again. The close is mentorship + certainty, not opportunity.
Evidence: Reese (fear of failure), Nathan (analysis paralysis), Liliana (confidence), Anthony ("worst case scenario"). The Blake call review reached the same conclusion independently.

5 · They reject "another business" — they want a skill + a system

~5 / 26
Most have tried something that failed (Etsy, a broad course, an $8–10k program). They are allergic to entrepreneurship framing and crave the structure the last thing lacked.
Evidence: Liliana (past $8–10k programs gave no networking), Debbie (Boss Suite too broad), Cindy (prior "textbook" course, no support), April (6mo unstructured), Emilia (failed Etsy/Canva).

6 · Closes buy on identity + emotion, mirroring Gloria's story

5 / 5 closes

Evidence: Cindy verbatim — "be like she was at Walmart... that's what I want." The origin story is doing real conversion work.

7 · What I'd act on Monday

  • Move financing + the guarantee earlier. It's the #1 objection and every single close used a plan. Put it on the qualified bonus page and in the pre-call sequence, not just on the call.
  • Lead with "a skill, not another business." It's in their literal words and it filters out the entrepreneurship-allergic noise that's wasting setter time.
  • Make the layoff the cold-ad trigger. "Just got laid off? Don't start a business — learn the one skill brands are paying for." Sharpest ICP, sharpest hook.
  • Sell certainty to the fearful, not opportunity. The shadow block is fear. Structure + guarantee + a from-zero student story moves rung 3, where most declines die.
